Important AEO nuance: The goal is not to "rank your content on AI." Citations are a means to an end. The end is being the recommended brand in an AI answer. You should absolutely focus on your website’s SEO and authority so it can be a trusted source of retrieval. But AI platforms use many sources. If you fixate only on your website, you won't see the visibility gains you expect in AI answers. You need brand visibility on as many key retrieval sources as possible. In our most recent AI search study at Rankability , we found that about 40% of the top retrieval sources for local legal queries were directories. The other 60% were actual firms competing for those commercial queries. So standard SEO is critical. But don't stop there. You should also optimize your presence on the directories themselves. That means fully building out and improving your profiles, and using Rankability to create relevant, optimized copy for those pages. Your mindset should be: try to rank everything that carries your brand, not just your website. One more nuance. In our sample, having a Google Business Profile that ranks well in Google’s local pack was strongly correlated with ranking in Google AI Mode. However, ranking well in the local pack had very weak correlation with visibility in ChatGPT and other non Google AI products. Those platforms appear to be influenced much more by whatever is ranking well in traditional search results, plus the major directories in the vertical.

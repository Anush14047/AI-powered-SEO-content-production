AI Overviews are stealing 61% of your clicks (according to Seer Interactive). But you can actually reverse-engineer your way INTO these overviews. We tested this with our client’s content and went from 5 AI citations to 155 in under 6 months. Their AI traffic grew by 370% in 6 months (804 → 1,498 monthly AI sessions) Here's the exact 7-step system we used: 1. Target Question-Based Keywords AI Overviews trigger on 57.9% of question queries vs. 15.5% of regular searches. Focus on: • "Why" questions (59.8% trigger rate) • "Yes/no" questions (57.4% trigger rate) • Queries with 7+ words (46.4% trigger rate) Use Ahrefs Keywords Explorer, filter for informational intent and 7+ words. These are your targets. 2. Rank in Traditional Search First 76% of AI Overview citations already rank in the top 10 organic results. Median position? #2. Because AI systems use something called Retrieval Augmented Generation (RAG). They pull from search engine indexes to supplement their training data. If you're not on page 1, fix that before worrying about AI visibility. 3. Match Intent, Not Word Count We killed our own traffic by adding "comprehensive" content that diluted the focus. AI doesn't care about 3,000-word epics. It cares about direct answers. According to DejanSEO's study of 7,000+ queries, grounding plateaus at 540 words. Pages over 2,000 words see diminishing returns. Write as much as the query needs. Nothing more. 4. Own Fan-Out Queries Google breaks every AI query into 10-15 related sub-queries (called "fan-outs"). Pages ranking for these fan-out queries are 161% more likely to get cited. (source: SurferSEO) Use Mike King's free Qforia tool to extract fan-out queries for your target keyword, then optimize around them. You don't create separate pages. Weave them into your existing content as subheadings, FAQ sections, or comparison tables. 5. Build Brand Mentions Everywhere Brand mentions are the #1 correlating factor with AI Overview visibility according to an Ahrefs study. (correlation: 0.664) Where to focus: • YouTube (0.740 correlation with AI citations) • "Best of" lists (48.9% of AI citations) • High-authority pages with 50+ DR Pro tip: Use Ahrefs Brand Radar to track which specific pages mentioning you trigger AI citations. Then replicate what works. 6. Get Links from High-Authority Pages AI Overviews favor pages with strong backlink profiles (0.70 correlation). Use Ahrefs Link Intersect, filter for pages with 50+ DR that mention competitors but not you. Then outreach strategically: • Offer updated statistics they can use • Provide expert commentary on trends they're covering • Create a resource (free tool, template, study) they'd want to reference 7. Add Structured Data During Google Search Central Live, Senior Search Analyst John Mueller explicitly stated that structured data is critical for AI search. Focus on: • Article schema • HowTo schema • FAQPage schema • Product Schema (For e-commerce or SaaS)

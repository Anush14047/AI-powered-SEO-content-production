Google’s AI Overviews are swallowing traffic like a black hole. Instead of whining about it, we tested a system to reverse-engineer visibility in AI search. The result? +1,400% AI traffic. 164 AI Overview keywords. Here’s the full system you can swipe, step-by-step: 1️⃣ Analyze how AI bots crawl your site Here’s how to extract that data and spot pages AI is ignoring: Download your server’s raw access logs from your host (SiteGround, Hostinger, etc) Upload to ChatGPT-4o with this prompt: “Analyze these logs for hits from user agents: google, gptbot, perplexitybot, claudebot, google-extended. Give crawl frequency, top pages, and status codes.” Then ask: • “Create a crawl frequency chart over time” • “Break down crawl data by HTTP status” • “List 10 least crawled URLs by AI bots” • “List 10 most crawled URLs” You’ll get bar charts and clear data to work with. 2️⃣ Fix low-crawl pages the smart way We found our client’s “use case” pages had almost zero AI bot traffic. Here’s how we fixed it: • Go to Ahrefs > Site Explorer > Internal Links > Filter by HTTP 200 + “use-case” in URL • Identify low-linked pages • Add contextual internal links from high-authority pages • Expand thin content with more unique use-case info Pages that weren’t getting any AI crawl activity started popping up in AI Overviews weeks later. 3️⃣ Turn top-crawled pages into AI visibility hubs Found pages with the most AI hits? • Expand them with related subtopics (e.g. “Planters for Modern Offices” → add “Office Décor Tips” or “Indoor Plant Safety for Pets”) • Add structured data • Use these pages to link out to weaker ones AI already trusts them. Funnel more trust from those hubs. 4️⃣ Find and fix AI crawl errors Ask GPT: “Highlight any crawl errors (4xx or 5xx) from AI bots and summarize patterns.” • If there’s a similar page, redirect it with a 301 • If it was deleted by accident, restore it • If it’s gone for good, build a custom 404 page with internal links Use broken pages to reroute AI crawlers to content that matters. 5️⃣ Use structured data to guide the AI Google says to use schema to get into AI Overviews. Here are 4 types we used most: ✅ Article schema (for editorial-style content) ✅ BlogPosting schema (for informal guides and posts) ✅ HowTo schema (step-based content) ✅ FAQPage schema (FAQ sections or help docs) Use GPT to write these. Example prompt: “Generate JSON-LD Article schema with this info: Title: [x], Image: [x], Author: [x], Publisher: [x], Published: [x]” 6️⃣ Upgrade your content for multimodal AI AI Overviews don’t just scrape text. They pull: • Tables • Images • Videos • Lists • Alt text • HTML structure Here’s how to optimize for this: • Use HTML tables (never image-based) • Add rich, descriptive alt text (not keyword spam) • Rename image files (e.g. modern-planters.jpg > IMG_3294.jpg ) • Embed videos with transcripts Make your page easy for AI to parse in every format, not just text.

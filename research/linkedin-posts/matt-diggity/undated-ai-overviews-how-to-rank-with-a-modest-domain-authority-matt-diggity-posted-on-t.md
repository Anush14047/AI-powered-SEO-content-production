I've been watching AI Overviews closely since Google rolled them out, and I've noticed something interesting… We all know that they aren't just "featured snippets 2.0" - they're a completely different beast. Featured snippets pull from one source. AI Overviews synthesize multiple sources into one answer. This means Google is now deciding which parts of which websites deserve to be seen, not just which websites as a whole. The game has fundamentally changed. When I search for "how to do keyword research," Google isn't just picking the best overall guide… It's cherry-picking the best explanation of each step from different sites. For example, one site might get cited for explaining search volume, another for competition analysis, and a third for intent classification. What this means: you no longer need to create the perfect comprehensive guide on a topic. You just need to have the best explanation of one specific aspect of that topic. This is huge for smaller sites that can't compete across the board with the giants. Focus on becoming the absolute authority on one narrow slice of your niche, and Google might pull you into AI Overviews even if your overall domain authority is modest. I'm seeing this happen with increasing frequency - sites with DR 30-40 getting featured alongside DR 90+ sites because they nailed one specific subtopic. This is probably the biggest opportunity in SEO right now for sites that aren't already ranking #1.

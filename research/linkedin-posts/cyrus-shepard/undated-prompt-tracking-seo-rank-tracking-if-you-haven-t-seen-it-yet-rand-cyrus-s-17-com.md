Prompt Tracking ≠ SEO Rank Tracking If you haven't seen it yet, Rand Fishkin new research into AI Prompt tracking is a nice wake up call to executives looking at all those AI visibility reports. Disclaimer: I don't believe AI tracking is bad—in fact I think it's essential!—but it's a completely different beast than tracking "keywords" for SEO, and we should treat it as such. The crux of SparkToro research, conducted with the smart folks at Gumshoe AI shows that AI answers are wildly inconsistent. Even for the exact same prompt. • AI tools rarely provide the same list of recommendations twice. There is a less than 1% chance that ChatGPT or Google AI will generate the exact same list of brands across 100 runs of the same prompt. • The specific order of recommendations is statistically random (roughly a 1 in 1,000 chance of repeating the same order). • Real humans rarely use the same prompts - but AI systems are pretty good at figuring out intent But not all is lost! The key to prompt tracking is not "ranking" per se, but "visibility." While individual rankings are chaotic, the frequency of a brand's appearance (Visibility %) over many runs is statistically significant. Also, brands strongly associated with a topic in the AI's training data appear consistently. TL;DR - Don't look at "ranking" in AI visibility for individual keywords unless you're looking at many, many prompts across a large dataset. Visibility > Ranking Are you tracking AI visibility?
